Optimized Sub-Sampling for Fast MRI Segmentation Using U-Net Architecture

Ashu Raman      Vani Yadav     

apr17@duke.edu     vani.yadav@duke.edu

Paper PDF

Magnetic resonance continues to be one of the strongest forms of imaging in diagnosis of disease processes. However, MR scans can be quite expensive and take a long time to acquire properly, serving as a drawback for patients. Thus, there is a need for a more effective way of sub-sampling frequency data so that scan times can be reduced without compromising the ability to still properly segment the features. Convolutional neural networks serve as a potential avenue for this sampling optimization, as they can be trained to choose the most important columns of frequency data, which reduces average scan time. This paper implements a U-Net to develop an automatic way of segmenting brain images for tumors, while also optimizing sub-sampling of the frequency domain so that only those frequencies that are important to recreating accurate segmentation masks are chosen. The U-Net is progressively made to choose fewer and fewer columns of k-space in order to segment tumors from brain images. In this study, we found that the CNN prefers to sample areas around DC, as well as some higher frequencies, in order to segment properly.
